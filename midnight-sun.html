Midnight Sun Design Team

Solar Innovation, Teamwork, and Real-World Engineering

About the Team & Project

The Midnight Sun Solar Vehicle Team is a completely student-run engineering initiative where the design, manufacturing, and racing of an entirely solar-powered vehicle occur every two years. This project, spearheaded by the University of Waterloo, showcases the innovative spirit and technical prowess of its students.

In 2023, the team focused on the hardware and mechanics for the eleventh iteration of the car, known as Car XI. The team consists of several specialties and ways to contribute to the vehicle's development, ranging from aerodynamics and mechanical design to software and hardware engineering.

Technical Contributions & Achievements
  • Designed custom PCBs in Altium, optimizing power distribution networks (PDN) and signal integrity for high-reliability automotive use.
  • Engineered a multilayer PCB stack-up for efficient telemetry, power management, and robust sensor integration.
  • Optimized solar panel orientation and battery management using advanced MPPT algorithms, increasing energy efficiency by 15% over previous designs.
  • Integrated a CAN Bus system for real-time communication between sensors, battery management, and the ECU, enabling rapid diagnostics and data-driven performance tuning.
  • Conducted EMI/EMC analysis to ensure compliance with automotive safety standards and minimize interference, using simulation and real-world testing.

My work extended to developing firmware for microcontrollers that managed sensor data acquisition, fault detection, and telemetry streaming. I collaborated with the software team to build a custom dashboard for live vehicle diagnostics, leveraging Python and C++ for backend data processing and visualization. Rigorous bench and on-road testing cycles were performed to validate system reliability under race conditions, including thermal cycling, vibration, and electromagnetic stress tests.

The result was a highly integrated, energy-efficient, and robust electrical system that contributed to the team's success in international solar vehicle competitions. This experience deepened my expertise in embedded systems, PCB design, and cross-disciplinary engineering teamwork.

My Role

As a core member of the Midnight Sun Design Team, I contributed to the design and development of solar-powered vehicles for international competitions. My primary focus was on electrical systems, PCB design, and system integration. Working alongside a multidisciplinary team, I gained hands-on experience in both hardware and software, collaborating to solve complex engineering challenges.

My responsibilities included schematic capture, PCB layout, and testing, as well as coordinating with mechanical and software teams to ensure seamless integration. I also participated in design reviews and technical presentations.
